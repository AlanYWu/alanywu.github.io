A multimodal OCR + LLM pipeline that takes scanned Chinese braille and produces
readable, tone-correct Chinese, so visually impaired students can produce text
their sighted teachers can grade.

The hard part is tone. Standard Chinese braille drops tone marks for speed,
which makes traditional rule-based decoding ambiguous about 90% of the time.
A learned context model brings that confusion rate to under 5%.

Recognized as a **Spotlight Project** at the NeurIPS High School Project Track
(top 20 of 335 submissions). Mentored independent research at Peking University.

Preprint: [arXiv:2407.06048](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.06048).
